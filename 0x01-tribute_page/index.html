Arsène Wenger

The legend who changed perceptions of the football sport and profession in England and abroad

Here's a timeline of Arsène Wenger, Le Professeur:

  • 1949 - Born in Strasbourg, Alsace, eastern France.
  • 1969 - Joins AS Mutzig, a club in the French Third Division.
  • 1971 - Enrolls at the University of Strasbourg to study politics and economics.
  • 1974 - Graduates with a degree in economics.
  • 1981 - Receives his manager's diploma in Paris.
  • 1983 - Becomes assistant coach for Ligue 2 side Cannes.
  • 1984 - Accepts the offer to become manager of AS Nancy.
  • 1987 - AS Nancy is relegated and Wenger leaves by mutual consent to join AS Monaco as manager.
  • 1988 - AS Monaco wins the French league in Wenger's debut season.
  • 1992 - Monaco finishes runners up to Werder Bremen in the European Cup Winners' Cup Final.
  • 1994 - Wenger agrees to manage Japanese club Nagoya Grampus Eight.
  • 1995 - Wenger receives the J. League Manger of the Year award.
  • 1996 - Appointed as Arsenal Manager after the dismissal of manager Bruce Rioch.
  • 1998 - Becomes the first foreign manager to lift the double beating Manchester United and Newcastle United to win the Premiership and FA Cup respectively.
  • 1999 - Signs Thierry Henry.
  • 2002 - Arsène lifts the league and FA cup double after a penultimate game against Manchester United at Old Trafford.
  • 2004 - The team wins the Premiership without a single defeat.
  • 2005 - The title goes to Chelsea, but Arsenal defeats Manchester United in the FA Cup.
  • 2006 - Arsenal leave Highbury for the newly built Emirates Stadium of which Arsène oversees the construction of.
  • 2014 - After a period of failure, Arsenal lifts the FA Cup after beating Hull City in a comback win.
  • 2015 - Once again Arsenal see FA Cup success, though league success continues to be far away.
  • 2017 - Attains another FA Cup success by beating Chelsea after a season full of turmoil.
  • 2018 - Arsène Wenger announces his departure from Arsenal Football Club.
  • 2019 - Becomes FIFA's Chief of Global Football Development.
